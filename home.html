Many people start their day with a cup of coffee.

I always wonder how people interact with/feel about this interface.

Sketch of a "basic" coffee maker

Key Components

  • Top lid: access to the filter cup and the water container
  • Filter cup: filtering coffee grounds
  • Water container: expecting water and sending it to the heating unit
  • Glass Coffee Holder: adding water to the container and holding hot coffee
    • Flip lid: allowing a large volume of water coming in/out
    • Handle: being held by the user
  • Warming plate: keeping coffee warm
  • Switch: turning on/off with a light indicator
  • Power chord: getting power

How do my users interact with it?

I observed and interviewed three people during and after they used the coffee maker.

Some of them use the coffee maker every day while some of them don't use it often.


Some patterns:

  • User A and user C both did some cleaning to the coffee maker/surrounding area.
  • User B and user C poured in coffee grounds fastly, while user 1 hesitated.
  • User B and user C did not focus on the coffee maker anymore after they flipped the switch.
  • User A pays more attention to the coffee maker than user B or user C does.

Questions/

  • What do you expect a coffee maker to do?
  • How often do you use a coffee maker?
  • Do you think it is easy to use?
  • What do you like about it?
  • What is something you find inconvenient?
  • Do you think there should be a sound notificaiton when brewing is done?
  • Would you consider investing in a fancier coffee machine?
  • If this coffee maker is broken, will you get the same one again?
  • What is an ideal version of a coffee machine for you?

Answers (A|B|C)

  • Make hot water or coffee | Make breakfast | Brew coffee
  • Never used it to make coffee | Pretty much everyday | Most days
  • It's not as easy as I thought | Sure | Yes
  • The design is very compact | Makes coffee without me doing much | *shrug* it works
  • A lot of steps to make it right | Nothing | It could be faster, maybe like those pod/capsule ones
  • No, but a visual indicator could be good | Not necessary | No
  • Yes for new experience | Maybe | Yea I want to try K-cup or Nespresso
  • No | Probably | I don't know, maybe a change
  • Easy to use, quieter | This one works for me | Clean, fast, visually pleasing, not too much work

Some notes:

  • User A mentioned several issues while user B and C found them to be trivial:
    • it's not easy to pour water from the coffee holder to the container
    • the noise is somewhat loud
    • it's not clear when it is done
  • User A and user C said they would clean the filter thoroughly
  • None of the users wanted a sound indicator, but user A mentioned that a timer/visual indicator could be good.
  • User A noticed more details about the coffee maker:
    • when I asked about the max volume of the container, user A answered it accurately
    • the others said they didn't pay attention or "the coffee holder" holds the proper amount of water
  • User B said they didn't care for fancy features, "the simpler the better".

Storyboard - A morning in the life of Busy Bruce

Bruce works as a software engineer - due to COVID-19 he has been working from home since March.

His old coffee maker is the first thing he goes to in the morning - it doesn’t even have the time setting feature, so he has to get out of bed to get his cup of joe.

This morning he wakes up 15 min before a stand-up meeting.

He rushes to the kitchen and lifts the top lid of the coffee maker, pulls out the filter cup, and dumps yesterday’s coffee grounds into the trash.

After a quick rinse, he pours a generous amount of coffee into the filter cup,

Quickly filling the coffee holder with tap water, he pours the water into the container. Flips the switch! He runs back to his desk to check Slack messages on his work laptop.

A customer’s server is down and his inbox is flooded with messages - he starts looking into the server log. 20 min later, the issue is resolved. He goes on to attend his meeting.

"Oh, coffee!" Finally he remembers his coffee, and he goes back to the kitchen.

Thanks to his coffee maker’s warming plate, his coffee is still hot.

He pours new coffee into his mug.

He turns off the switch and goes back to his desk.

Another day is saved by his good ole coffee maker!
